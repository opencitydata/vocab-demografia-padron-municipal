Introduction back to ToC

Vocabulary for the representation of the census data from the relation of inhabitants files exchanged by City Councils and Instituto Nacional de Estadística. These data correspond to microdata, raw data of each inhabitant, internally used by City Councils. In order to represent the census macrodata, aggregated data of inhabitants, several data cubes have been defined in the [Cubes of Census of Inhabitants](http://vocab.ciudadesabiertas.es/def/demografia/cubo-padron-municipal) vocabulary. This vocabulary has been developed in the context of the project "Plataforma de gobierno abierto, colaborativa e interoperable" (Open Government collaborative and interoperable platform), where the municipalities of Madrid, Zaragoza, Santiago de Compostela and A Coruña are involved. This vocabulary reuses terms from the [schema.org](http://schema.org/) vocabulary, which includes concepts and relationships used for similar purposes. In addition, it refers to terms from other vocabularies used for the representation of concepts in domains such as administrative divisions, postal address, and street. More specifically, it reuses the [schema.org](http://schema.org/) vocabulary to represent the inhabitants. It also reuses the [territory](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/sector-publico/territorio#) vocabulary to describe territorial entities in Spain, such as district, collective entity, neighborhood, etc., where the inhabitant resides. In addition, the [postal-address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/commons/direccion-postal#) vocabulary is also reused to describe the residence address of the inhabitant. The [postal-address](http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/commons/direccion-postal#) vocabulary also includes relations to the [callejero]( http://vocab.linkeddata.es/datosabiertos/def/urbanismo-infraestructuras/callejero#) vocabulary in order to represent details of the road where the inhabitant's home is located.
